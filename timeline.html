2019
December

31st December

China alerted the World Health Organisation about an unknown virus.

2020
January

1st January

Huanan Seafood Wholesale Market shut down by Chinese officials.

Infections exceeded 40 people.

7th January

Virus identified

Identified as belonging to the coronavirus family.

11th January

First announced death in the World

61-year-old man died of heart failure in China.

13th January

First international transmission

Reported a case in Thailand.

17th January

Second death in Wuhan

3 airports to start screening in the United States.

20th January

Transmission method confirmed as human-to-human.

23rd January

Effective Quarentine

for cities of Wuhan, Xiantao and Chibi in Hubei province.

30th January

WHO declares Global Emergency.

February

2nd February

1st death outside China.

A Chinese man from Wuhan died in Philippines.

6th February

Malaysia reported the country's first known human-to-human transmission.

Europe reached 30 infected.

7th February

Li Wenliang, a doctor who was one of the first to sound out about the virus.

Jail for Hong Kongers who breach quarentine rules.

9th February

Death toll surpasses SARS in China.

811 deaths, 37,198 infected.

10th February

China had 908 confirmed deaths and a total of 40,171 infections.

President Xi Jinping appeared in public, a first since the outbreak, urges confidence in battle against the virus.

13th February

1st death in Japan.

13th February

1st death in Europe, from France.

16th February

1st death in Taiwan.

19th February

Iran reports 2 deaths, hours after first confirmed case.

20th February

South Korea reports 1st death.

21th February

1st case in Israel.

3rd death in Italy, shuts down Venice Carnival early.

25th February

Iran's Deputy Health Minister Iraj Harirchi confirms he has Coronavirus.

27th February

US considers activating the Defence Protection Act.

Infections passed 82,000 worldwide, 2,800 deaths confirmed.

March

5th March

1st case in Saudi Arabia.

7th March

China's Health Commission reported 99 new cases, down from 143 cases the day before, with a total of 80,651 cases nationwide.

China's economy effectively halted.

8th March

Lockdown for Saudi Arabia, for eastern Qatif region

Announced the suspension of all schools and universities across the country.

Lockdown for Italy

Strict quarantine in the state of Lombardy and 14 other areas up north.

9th March

Iran releases 70,000 prisoners

in an effort to curb the spread of Coronavirus.

11th March

WHO declares pandemic.

15th March

Kazakhstan, the Philippines and Austria tightened restrictions.

18th March

"Human biosecurity emergency" declared by Australia.

Chile and Guatemala shut down their borders.

China reports, no cases, for the first time after the start of the outbreak.

20th March

More than 10,000 deaths confirmed.

25th March

US accounted for highest number of infections.

124,000 cases and 2,000 deaths.

27th March

Lockdown for India & South Africa.

April

5th April

Prime Minister Boris Johnson of the UK, admitted to hospital, 10 days after testing positive.

7th April

Japan declares State of Emergency, amid spike of cases.

Singapore declares Circuit Breaker, partial lockdown to curb infections.

15th April

2,000,000 infections confirmed worldwide

Global economy projected to shrink by 3%, biggest contraction since the Great Depression.

26th April

China announces that Wuhan has no more outstanding cases.

All who have been treated, discharged.

May

Many countries announce laxing of restrictions

Notably, Italy, Australia, France, Malaysia & Singapore.

As of current, ... people and counting, are still infected.
